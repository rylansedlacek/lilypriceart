Bio & Statement
Lily Price
Painting Drawing Sculpture

Artist Bio

Lily Price is a painter and a sculptor based in Fredericksburg, Virginia. She is currently enrolled at the University of Mary Washington, where she is pursuing degrees in Studio Art and Business, with an expected graduation date in Spring 2026. Price primarily focuses on oil paintings that vary from abstract to hyperrealistic. In her sculptures, the artist utilizes found materials to create her large sculptures. The practice in realism has shifted to focusing on conceptual ideas to create abstract paintings and sculptures. Price’s goal within her artistic endeavors is to fulfill her deep desire for creation by any means necessary.

Artist Statement

Art has always been a constant in my life. My work doesn’t stem from a single concept or innate talent but emerges from an instinctive need to create. In every piece, my creative drive is reflected through my desire to highlight detail. I aim to create a feeling of discomfort in my work. I want to evoke a sense of shock in the viewer, whether that comes from the subject matter, my use of physical materials, or showcasing my technical skills — like blending, controlling brush strokes, and creating 3D imagery. When working in three dimensions, I follow a more conceptual process: an idea takes shape in my mind, and I translate it into physical form using materials that carry meaning within the concept. I often work with textiles and drapery like yarn, fabric, and cardboard, valuing their flexibility and the way they invite movement. I enjoy creating sculptures that respond to touch or motion, allowing the materials to react and engage with their surroundings physically. In my paintings and drawings, I draw inspiration from art history, studying the techniques of the old masters to deepen my understanding of realism and technical craftsmanship. Through this exploration, I aim to bridge historical influences with my personal voice as an artist. The act of creating provides a sense of fulfillment for me; I am able to feel totally centered when I am painting or sculpting.
